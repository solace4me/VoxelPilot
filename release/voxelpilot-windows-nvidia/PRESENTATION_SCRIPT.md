# VoxelPilot Live Demo Script

Use this as a short 3 to 5 minute presentation script for the NVIDIA laptop demo.

## Opening

"This is `VoxelPilot`, a presentation-ready CUDA volume exploration workstation built for NVIDIA hardware. The goal was not just to render a volume, but to create a complete interactive review experience that combines 3D rendering, orthogonal slice inspection, measurements, capture, and guided presentation tools in one app."

## Why It Matters

"A lot of medical volume rendering projects stop at showing that the GPU can render a dataset. What makes this one different is that it is shaped like a usable workstation. It helps the presenter or reviewer move through a volume, explain what they are seeing, measure points of interest, and save the session output."

## Launch Moment

"On startup, the app presents a branded splash screen and moves into demo mode. That is intentional because this build is meant to be shown live, not just tested privately."

## Main Interface

"The main workspace is organized around a real-time CUDA render view, supported by a control panel, slice review, metadata, help, and about panels. So instead of jumping between separate tools, everything needed for the demo is already in one place."

## Volume Loading

"From here I can load a raw volume directly on the machine. The app also reports import status, expected dataset size, and updates metadata once the volume is available."

## 3D And Slice Review

"The core interaction is the combination of the 3D render and the orthogonal slices. I can orbit around the volume in real time, switch to preset views like Front or Isometric, and then cross-check the anatomy through sagittal, coronal, and axial slices."

## Review Controls

"This build also includes transfer mapping, clipping, histogram insight, lighting, render resolution control, and workspace presets. That makes it useful both for technical rendering review and for repeatable presentations."

## Measurement And Capture

"To make the app practical, I can place measurement points, read out distances in voxel and world units, export a PNG snapshot, and save the whole workspace session for later reuse."

## What Makes It Stand Out

"The biggest selling point is that this is not just a CUDA renderer. It is a complete, demo-ready exploration workstation. It combines rendering performance, slice-based interpretation, measurement, export, guided help, and presentation flow in one application."

## Close

"So the key idea behind VoxelPilot is simple: take the power of CUDA volume rendering and wrap it in a polished interface that is actually ready to explore, explain, and present."
