# VoxelPilot Pitch Deck Outline

Use this outline for a short slide deck when presenting the project.

## Slide 1: Title

- VoxelPilot
- NVIDIA Volume Explorer for Local Laptop Demos
- Short tagline:
  Presentation-ready CUDA volume exploration workstation

## Slide 2: Problem

- Many CUDA volume rendering projects prove rendering performance but stop short of usable workflow.
- Reviewers and presenters often need more than a 3D image:
  slice context, measurements, export, repeatable sessions, and clear demo flow.

## Slide 3: Solution

- VoxelPilot merges rendering and review into one local NVIDIA-laptop application.
- It combines:
  real-time volume rendering, slice review, measurement, capture, presets, and guided presentation support.

## Slide 4: Core Features

- Real-time CUDA volume rendering
- Orthogonal slice review
- Transfer mapping and clipping
- Histogram and metadata insight
- Measurement in voxels and world units
- PNG snapshot export
- Workspace session save/load

## Slide 5: Demo-Ready Experience

- Branded startup splash
- Demo mode banner
- One-click guided walkthrough
- In-app Help and About panels
- Packaged launcher and laptop-ready release folder

## Slide 6: What Makes It Stand Out

- More than a renderer:
  it behaves like a compact review workstation.
- Combines 3D and 2D understanding:
  users inspect the rendered volume and validate it with slices.
- Presentation-ready:
  the app helps the presenter, not just the developer.

## Slide 7: Live Demo Flow

- Launch on NVIDIA laptop
- Load a volume
- Switch views and orbit in 3D
- Review axial, coronal, sagittal slices
- Place a measurement
- Save a snapshot
- Save a reusable session

## Slide 8: Next Version

- Richer viewport-based annotations
- Better metadata import workflows
- AI-assisted guidance and exploration support
- More advanced measurement and review tools

## Slide 9: Closing

- VoxelPilot is a presentation-ready CUDA volume exploration workstation, not just a renderer.
- Built to explore, explain, and demonstrate volume data on local NVIDIA hardware.
